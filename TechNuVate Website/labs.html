What Is Labs

The Gap Between Learning and Working Is Real. Labs Closes It.

Most people finish a course and still feel stuck. They have the knowledge but they have never applied it under real conditions, with real deadlines, real feedback, and real expectations. That is the gap TechNuVate Labs was built to close.

In Labs, you work on structured project briefs, get feedback from experienced mentors, build a portfolio that shows what you can actually do, and get evaluated to a professional standard. By the time you leave Labs, you are not just a graduate. You are someone who has delivered.

How Labs Works

Three Stages. One Clear Outcome.

Labs is structured to move you through a progression, from orientation to active project work to final evaluation. Each stage has a purpose.

01
🎯
Orientation and Brief Assignment

You are introduced to Labs standards, expectations, and how professional work is evaluated. You are then assigned your first project brief, a real-world task that mirrors what clients and employers actually ask for in your track.

02
🔧
Active Project Work and Mentorship

You work on your brief with access to structured mentor check-ins and peer review sessions. Your work is reviewed against professional standards, not just whether it is done, but whether it is done well. Feedback is specific and actionable.

03
🏆
Final Review and Portfolio Sign-Off

Your completed project is evaluated by a mentor. Work that meets the standard gets added to your official TechNuVate portfolio. The best performers across all tracks are flagged for deployment on Studio client projects.

What You Actually Do

Not Assignments. Real Work.

Everything you do in Labs is designed to reflect what real employers and clients expect from someone in your track.

📄
Work on Real Client Briefs

Project briefs are drawn from real or realistic client scenarios. You are solving an actual problem, not an exercise. The scope, the constraints, and the delivery expectations all reflect real work.

💬
Receive Structured Mentor Feedback

Every submission gets reviewed by a mentor who has worked in your field. Feedback is not just "good job" or "try again." It is specific, actionable, and delivered to help you grow in the areas that matter most.

👥
Collaborate in Peer Review Sessions

You review each other's work and give structured feedback. This builds your ability to evaluate quality, communicate professionally, and understand how your work looks through someone else's eyes.

📋
Build a Professional Portfolio

Work that passes the standard is documented into your TechNuVate portfolio. This is a real record of what you have done, not a certificate, not a course description. Actual work, properly presented.

🌟
Get Evaluated Against Professional Standards

Every piece of work is assessed the same way a client or employer would assess it. You learn what "professional quality" actually means in your track and what it takes to consistently deliver at that level.

🚀
Get Noticed for Studio Deployment

The best performers in Labs are flagged for real Studio client projects. This is how talent moves from Labs into actual paid work. Your performance in Labs is your application.

Mentorship in Labs

You Are Not Working Alone. You Have People Behind You.

Mentors in Labs are not teachers delivering lectures. They are professionals who have done the work you are learning to do. Their job is to review your output, push your standards, and help you understand what separates good work from great work.

Every Labs participant has access to mentor feedback at each stage of their project. You bring the effort. Mentors bring the standard.

🔍
Detailed Work Reviews

Mentors go through your submission line by line, decision by decision, and explain exactly what works, what does not, and why.

📈
Progress Check-ins

Scheduled check-ins during the project let you ask questions, clarify direction, and make sure you are not stuck going the wrong way.

🎯
Career Positioning Guidance

Mentors help you understand how to present your portfolio, how to talk about your experience, and what the market in your track actually looks for.

Industrial Training

Students on IT Placement? Labs Is the Right Place.

If you are a university or polytechnic student in your industrial training (IT) period, TechNuVate Labs gives you a placement that actually means something. Do your entire IT from the comfort of your state. No relocation. No transport stress. No feeding allowance to worry about. Just real digital work matched to your course, a mentor guiding you through it, and a properly stamped and signed logbook at the end.

🏫
Who Is Eligible

Students currently in their IT period at university or polytechnic level, studying any course that allows you to complete an internship in a digital, creative, or business environment. This includes Marketing, Mass Communication, Business Administration, Accounting, Journalism, Computer Science, and any other course whose IT requirements can be fulfilled through digital skills training and real project work.

📋
What You Will Do

Work on real digital project briefs matched to your discipline. Marketing students do marketing work. Mass Communication students handle content and media. Management students support project and operations briefs.

📄
What You Walk Away With

A TechNuVate Labs completion letter for your IT supervisor, a portfolio of real work you completed during your placement, and a professional reference you can use as you start your career.

Important Notice on Logbook Submission

We are currently accepting IT placement students from Rivers State, Bayelsa State, Abia State and Akwa Ibom State.

At the end of your placement, you will waybill your completed logbook to our Port Harcourt office. We sign, stamp, and return it to you. All waybill costs are handled by the student.

If you are from another state not listed above and you are open to handling the logistics yourself, you are welcome to reach out and we will take it from there.
